The Solution

Straddle-Beam Monorail System

An elevated monorail running above the road median, requiring zero road surface area and providing grade-separated mass transit.

△

Elevated Guideway

Pre-stressed concrete beam supported by Y-shaped piers at 25-30m intervals. 8-12m clearance above road for heavy vehicle passage.

⚡

Electric Power Supply

750V DC third rail integrated into the guideway beam with overhead catenary backup. Zero local emissions.

■

Concrete Support Piers

Reinforced concrete Y-columns placed in the road median. Foundation footprint of only 2m x 2m per pier.

★

Passenger Stations

8 elevated stations with platform screen doors, escalators, lifts, and covered waiting areas. 3-minute headway in peak hours.

⚐

Safety Barriers

Crash-rated barriers around pier bases, pedestrian fencing along the guideway, and anti-climb protection at stations.

⇄

Road User Protection

Pier impact barriers rated to MASH TL-4 standard. Drainage integration to prevent water pooling. Clear signage and lighting.

Technical

System Specifications

Based on proven straddle-beam monorail technology (Hitachi, BYD SkyRail, Bombardier INNOVIA class).

System TypeStraddle-Beam Monorail (rubber-tyred)
Guideway BeamPre-stressed concrete, 850mm wide, 1500mm deep, PC steel strand
Support PiersReinforced concrete Y-columns, 25-30m span, 2m x 2m footing
Track Gauge850mm beam width (straddle configuration)
Clearance Height8-12m above road surface (min. 5.5m for vehicle passage)
Power Supply750V DC third rail + overhead catenary backup
Train Consist4-car articulated, 60m length, 800 passengers (crush load)
Maximum Speed80 km/h (design) / 65 km/h (commercial average)
CapacityUp to 40,000 passengers per hour per direction (pphpd)
Headway3 min peak / 6 min off-peak / 90 sec minimum design
SignallingCBTC (Communications-Based Train Control) with ATO/ATP
Safety SystemsPlatform screen doors, CCTV, fire suppression, emergency walkway
Road ProtectionMASH TL-4 crash barriers, pedestrian fencing, LED warning lights

Global Precedents

Proven Monorail Systems Worldwide

Straddle-beam monorails operate successfully in major cities across Asia and South America.

JP

Tokyo Monorail, Japan

17.8 km, opened 1964. Carries 127,000 passengers daily. Connects Haneda Airport to central Tokyo. 60+ years of reliable service.

MY

KL Monorail, Malaysia

8.6 km, 11 stations. Part of integrated transit network. 170,000 daily riders. Proved monorail viability in tropical developing-country context.

BR

São Paulo Line 15, Brazil

26.6 km when complete. Bombardier INNOVIA 300 trains. Serves dense urban corridors. Cost: $62M/km. Most relevant comparator.

IN

Mumbai Monorail, India

19.5 km, 17 stations. Built in dense urban environment with minimal land acquisition. Cost: $53M/km. Developing-country benchmark.

CN

Chongqing Monorail, China

98.5 km combined (Lines 2 & 3). World's longest and busiest monorail network. 1.2 million daily passengers. Navigates extreme terrain.

EG

Cairo Monorail, Egypt

98.5 km (under construction). Africa's first monorail. Alstom INNOVIA. $4.5B. Directly relevant as African precedent for Uganda.
